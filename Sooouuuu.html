Taha

Taha is a chapter (Surah) in the Quran, specifically the 20th chapter. It is named after the Arabic letters "Ta" and "Ha" which appear at the beginning of the chapter. The Surah contains 135 verses (Ayahs) and is classified as a Meccan Surah, meaning it was revealed to the Prophet Muhammad while he was in Mecca.

The chapter primarily focuses on the story of Prophet Moses (Musa) and his mission to free the Israelites from the tyranny of Pharaoh. It highlights various aspects of Moses' life, including his early years, his encounter with God at the burning bush, and his confrontations with Pharaoh. The Surah also emphasizes the importance of faith, patience, and reliance on God in the face of adversity$.
